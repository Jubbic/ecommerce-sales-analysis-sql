# E-Commerce Sales Analysis Using MySQL

## Business Problem

An e-commerce company wants to better understand customer purchasing behavior, product performance, and revenue trends to improve business decision-making and identify growth opportunities.

As a Data Analyst, I used MySQL to analyze transactional sales data and build reports that answer key business questions related to customer value, sales performance, and category profitability.

---

## Project Objectives

- Identify high-value customers
- Analyze customer spending patterns
- Measure product and category performance
- Track revenue trends over time
- Segment customers based on spending behavior
- Create executive-level business reports

---

## Database Tables

- Customers
- Orders
- Order_Items
- Products
- Categories

---

## Business Questions Answered

### 1. Customer Spending Report
Ranks customers based on total spending and total number of orders.

### 2. Top Customer in Each City
Identifies the highest-spending customer within every city.

### 3. Monthly Sales Trend Analysis
Uses SQL window functions to compare order revenue against previous orders.

### 4. Revenue by Product Category
Determines which product categories generate the most revenue.

### 5. Customer Segmentation
Classifies customers into:
- High Value
- Medium Value
- Low Value

based on total spending.

### 6. Final Business Report
Combines customer spending, order counts, spending ranks, and customer segments into a single executive report.

---

## Key Insights

### Customer Analysis
- Identified top-spending customers across all cities.
- Ranked customers based on total revenue generated.
- Segmented customers into High, Medium, and Low Value groups.

### Revenue Analysis
- Determined which product categories generated the highest revenue.
- Measured revenue contribution by category.

### Trend Analysis
- Compared order revenue against previous orders using SQL window functions.
- Identified periods of revenue growth and decline.

### Business Reporting
Built a consolidated customer performance report combining:
- Total Orders
- Total Spending
- Spending Rank
- Customer Segment
